Macroeconomics    Basics

Macroeconomics is a branch of economics that studies how an overall economy—the market systems that operate on a large scale—behaves. Macroeconomics studies economy-wide phenomena such as inflation, price levels, rate of economic growth, national income, gross domestic product (GDP), and changes in unemployment. We can have some elementary knowledge about macroeconomics through three aspects: schools of thought, macroeconomic concepts and macroeconomic policy.
